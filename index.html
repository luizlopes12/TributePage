A Timeline of Bob Marley's Career

  • 1945 Born in Nine Miles, St. Ann's, Jamaica

  • 1962 Left school at 14 to concentrate on music

  • 1963 Formed the Wailing Rudeboys (later becoming The Wailin' Wailers)

  • 1964 The Wailers's first single, 'Simmer Down', reaches Number 1 in Jamaica's JBC Radio

  • 1966 Marries Rita (Alpharita) Anderson, The next day he leaves his new bride and their first child to head to Wilmington, Delaware, USA, where he joins his mother.

  • 1966 Marley returns to Jamaica with some savings, setting up a record store in Kingston with Rita

  • 1967 Reunites with Peter Tosh and Bunny Livingston

  • 1968 Composition ('Stir It Up') and Danny Sims, Nash's manager, signs Marley to Nash's JAD label

  • 1968 Marley has begun exploring Rastafarianism

  • 1969 Along with the rest of the Wailers, Marley fully embraces Rastafarianism

  • 1970 Debut Wailers album "Soul Rebel" released

  • 1972 After a difficult, though creative period, The Wailers, finding themselves alone in London and effectively left to their own devices by Johnny Nash and Danny Sims, sign with Island Records's boss Chris Blackwell.

  • 1973 The Wailers appear at Max's Kansas City Club in New York (the centre of American new wave activity a year or so later), supporting Bruce Springsteen.

  • 1974 Eric Clapton reaches Number 1 in the US singles charts with his cover version of Marley's 'I Shot The Sheriff', taken from Clapton's 461 Ocean Boulevard'

  • 1975 The new Wailers play two classic dates at the Lyceum London, immortalised later that year on the album "Live!" from which a liver version of 'No Woman No Cry' is also released.

  • 1976 The Wailers play the Roxy, Los Angeles-a concert later listed in Rolling Stone's 1987 feature Live! Twenty concerts that changed rock'n'roll. "Rastaman Vibrations" released.
